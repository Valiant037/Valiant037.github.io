Atomic Trial (2017)

- educational VR game about the periodic table and chemical elements -

Overview

Atomic Trial is an educational VR experience designed to make learning the periodic table engaging and memorable. The project utilizes a "trial-based" gameplay loop where students must identify, combine, and interact with different chemical elements to solve puzzles within a futuristic laboratory setting.

My Role

In my role as Art Director, I supervised the art team in developing a cohesive sci-fi aesthetic that also served educational clarity. I oversaw the design of the elemental visualization system—ensuring each atom and molecule was represented in a way that was visually distinct and scientifically informative—while managing environmental lighting and UI consistency.

Highlights

  • Created a gamified "learning by doing" experience for chemistry education.
  • Designed stylized 3D models for atomic structures and laboratory environments.
  • Established a visual language for elemental properties and chemical reactions in VR.
  • Oversaw the production of promotional materials and project-wide art standards.

Art & Process

The visual direction aimed for "scientific futurism." We used sharp, clean lines and distinct element-specific color coding to help students navigate the periodic table intuitively. The lighting was designed to simulate a high-tech lab, using emissive surfaces to draw attention to interactive elements.
