Seáhuán (2024)

- immersive VR short film set in the Sino-French War -

Overview

Seáhuán is a cinematic VR short film that follows A-Huea into the Sino-French War, blending volumetric environments, motion capture, and spatial sound to immerse the viewer inside a historical moment.

My Role

As Director, I collaborated closely with the writer to develop the story and pacing, blocking camera paths in VR and defining how the viewer’s perspective moves through each scene. As Art Director, I oversaw shot composition, lighting, asset quality, and the final look of every frame.

Highlights

  • Combines cinematic framing with room-scale VR to guide the viewer’s attention.
  • Extensive motion capture direction for body performance and subtle character acting.
  • Careful control of pacing, camera travel, and scene transitions to avoid motion discomfort.
  • Built in Unreal Engine with a pipeline tuned for high-quality exhibition display.

Art & Process

This frame represents the tonal anchor for Seáhuán — establishing atmospheric depth, lighting direction, and character scale within the environment. Here I focus on how the composition leads the eye, and how color and value frameworks support the emotional beats of the story.
