Cell Factory (2017)

- educational VR game exploring the micro-world of cell anatomy -

Overview

Cell Factory is an educational VR game designed to teach students about cell anatomy and biological functions through an immersive, gamified experience. The project allows users to "shrink" down to a microscopic scale and explore the inner workings of a cell, interacting with various organelles to understand their roles.

My Role

As Art Director at Shadoworks Studio, I supervised the art team in creating a visually engaging yet biologically grounded environment. I defined the stylistic direction—balancing scientific accuracy with a vibrant, game-like aesthetic—and oversaw the design of interactive assets and environmental lighting.

Highlights

  • Developed an immersive educational environment focused on spatial learning.
  • Designed stylized 3D models of organelles that simplify complex biological structures for students.
  • Implemented interactive "active learning" mechanics within the VR space.
  • Optimized complex microscopic environments for smooth real-time performance.

Art & Process

The visual strategy was to make the microscopic world feel "alive" and approachable. We used organic, fluorescent color palettes and fluid shaders to simulate the cytoplasmic environment, ensuring that every interaction felt responsive and educationally relevant.
